WHY THIS EXISTS / LIVING PRACTICE

The Gradient exists so knowledge can remain usable without becoming flattened, trusted without becoming frozen, and revised without being erased.

Its purpose is not to make the world feel more controlled.

Its purpose is to help people steward complexity with better memory, clearer relationships, and more accountable judgment.

This is a system for people who need to keep thinking carefully after the first explanation breaks.

ONE CENTRAL CLAIM

Modern systems can store and retrieve. They struggle to preserve continuity: provenance, context, revision, and accountable judgment.

Interpretation (labeled): this is not primarily a technical problem. It is a constitutional optimization problem: what the system implicitly rewards, hides, and erases.

When continuity collapses, communities relearn under pressure; contradictory evidence disappears; interpretation detaches from lived reality.

ONE CENTRAL CLAIM

The Gradient governs evolution through commitments: admission, provenance, relationship, permanence of revision, and return to practice.

Operating commitments (summary): Admission precedes metabolization. Provenance precedes authority. Relationship precedes classification. Revision remains permanent. Nothing simply disappears. Practice returns to memory.

Interpretation (labeled): these commitments prevent two opposite failures: freezing knowledge into brittle canon, or revising until nothing has lineage.
