## Purpose

The Relationship explains the typed institutional link in The Gradient.

It is the object that connects Cards, Claims, Sources, Evidence, Practices, and History without making folders the primary model of organization.

## Constitutional Role

The Relationship protects meaning from being trapped in containers.

It makes knowledge traversable, inspectable, and reviewable across institutional objects.

It preserves the distinction between connection and proof.

It allows the institution to show how things relate without turning those relations into Canon, Validation, or final truth.

## Boundaries

The Relationship follows metabolization.

It is a derived institutional object.

It is not a Card.

It is not a Claim.

It is not raw source material.

It is not Evidence.

It is not a folder.

It is not a taxonomy tree.

It is not a database join table.

It is not a full knowledge graph.

It is not Canon.

It is not Validation.

It is not Promotion.

It does not prove the truth of the objects it connects.

It records a typed, reviewable connection between institutional objects.

## Diagram Family

Link / Connector Plate.

Primary structure: one central typed relationship object connecting two bounded institutional anchors.

Secondary structure: small grounding links to source, evidence, practice context, and review history.

Do not use a full knowledge graph.

Do not use a folder tree.

Do not use a taxonomy tree.

Do not use a database schema.

Do not use an app interface.

Do not use a dashboard.

## Visual Center

One severe central connector labeled `RELATIONSHIP`.

The connector sits between two bounded anchors labeled `FROM OBJECT` and `TO OBJECT`.

The relationship object contains the required relationship fields as internal facets, bands, or rows.

Small external tethers connect the relationship to:

- Source Link
- Evidence Link
- Practice Context
- Review History

The visual hierarchy must make the Relationship more important than the anchor objects and grounding tethers.

The plate must communicate typed connection, not storage, containment, folder nesting, or proof.

## Orientation

Landscape is preferred.

Near-square is acceptable if needed for field readability.

The orientation reason is lateral connection between bounded institutional objects, not threshold crossing, ledger readability, or conversion logic.
