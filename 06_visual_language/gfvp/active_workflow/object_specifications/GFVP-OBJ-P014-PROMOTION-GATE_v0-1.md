## Purpose

Promotion Gate explains the institutional threshold where validated material is considered for a higher status.

It determines whether material should remain validated, be promoted, be revised, be held, or be routed for constitutional review.

## Constitutional Role

Promotion protects The Gradient from confusing usefulness with institutional authority.

It makes status change explicit, reviewable, and reversible where appropriate.

It preserves the distinction between validated material, promoted institutional material, and canon.

## Boundaries

Promotion Gate considers status elevation.

It is not Validation.

It is not Canon.

It is not constitutional ratification.

It is not Admission.

It is not Source Review.

It is not image approval.

It does not make material permanently true.

It does not bypass steward review.

It may produce promoted status, hold status, revision route, or constitutional review route.

## Diagram Family

Gate / Threshold Plate.

Primary structure: one central Promotion Gate with Validated Object entering and status-elevation dispositions exiting.

Canon Boundary and Constitutional Review may appear only as subordinate routes, not as completed canon.

Do not render marketing promotion, career promotion, product launch, award badge, or software release pipeline.

## Visual Center

One severe central object labeled `PROMOTION GATE`.

The central gate should show validated material entering and distinct disposition exits.

The visual hierarchy must make status elevation reviewable and non-canonical by default.

## Orientation

Landscape is acceptable and likely appropriate for threshold crossing.

Portrait or near-square is acceptable if the disposition exits remain readable.

The orientation reason is status threshold movement, not product launch or software release flow.

No paper mockup, no shadows, no perspective, no UI screen, no dashboard, no app card, no social media promotion, no marketing campaign, no product launch, no sales funnel, no corporate ladder, no career ladder, no trophy, no award badge, no gold seal, no certification stamp, no software release pipeline, no colorful categories, no neon, no random glyphs, no prompt engineering, no QC document, no image-generation process, no canonical language, no final authority language, no invented metadata.
