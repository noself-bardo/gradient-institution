## Purpose

The Admission Gate explains the first institutional boundary an artifact must pass before entering The Gradient.

It determines whether material may enter with named identity, provenance, role, trust state, and next action.

## Constitutional Role

The Admission Gate protects the institution from anonymous, contextless, mislabeled, or prematurely authoritative material.

It preserves the rule that admission precedes metabolization.

It prevents downstream engines, archives, and visual systems from treating unreviewed material as institutional knowledge.

## Boundaries

The Admission Gate is an entry boundary.

It is not Source Review.

It is not Metabolization.

It is not Validation.

It is not Promotion.

It is not Canonization.

It does not decide truth.

It decides whether and how an artifact may enter the institution for appropriate next action.

## Inputs / Outputs

### Input

Artifact.

The artifact may be a prompt package, conversation, interview, book, sketch, photograph, engine, constitution, video, audio, research paper, personal journal, observation, dataset, or unknown-provenance material.

### Required Intake Fields

- What is it?
- Where did it come from?
- What is its intended role?
- What level of trust does it currently deserve?
- What should happen next?

### Outputs

- Admit
- Hold
- Reject
- Defer
- Request Review
- Archive
