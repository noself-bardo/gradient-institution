## Purpose

The Claim explains the internal reasoning atom in The Gradient.

It is a bounded interpretive assertion that may be linked to Cards, Sources, Evidence, Relationships, and Practices without becoming validated or canonical by default.

## Constitutional Role

The Claim protects institutional reasoning from dissolving into unstructured notes, folders, or vibes.

It makes interpretation inspectable, linkable, contestable, and revisable.

It preserves the distinction between human-facing Cards and internal reasoning structures.

## Boundaries

The Claim follows metabolization.

It is a derived institutional object.

It is not a Card.

It is not a Relationship.

It is not raw source material.

It is not Evidence.

It is not Source Review.

It is not a user-facing editing surface by default.

It is not Canon.

It is not Validation.

It is not Promotion.

It does not become true because it exists.

It records a bounded interpretive assertion with source grounding, trust state, and relationship context.

## Diagram Family

Reasoning Atom Plate.

Primary structure: one central atomic claim object.

Secondary structure: small grounding links to source, evidence, cards, relationships, counterclaims, and review history.

Do not use a full knowledge graph.

Do not use a folder tree.

Do not use a database schema.

Do not use an app interface.

Do not use a dashboard.

Do not use a legal brief.

## Visual Center

One severe central atomic object labeled `CLAIM`.

The claim object contains the required claim fields as internal facets or rows.

Small external tethers connect the claim to:

- Source Link
- Evidence Link
- Related Cards
- Related Relationships
- Counterclaims
- Review History

The tethers must show grounding and contestability without becoming a full network map.

The claim must feel like an internal institutional reasoning object, not a public-facing card or software UI element.

The visual hierarchy must make the Claim more important than its tethers.

## Orientation

Near-square is preferred.

Portrait is acceptable if needed for field readability.

Landscape is not preferred unless the claim anatomy becomes unreadable.

The orientation reason is reasoning-object anatomy and internal inspection, not threshold crossing, ledger readability, or conversion logic.
