## Purpose

Canon Boundary explains the strict boundary between material that is useful, admitted, validated, promoted, or historically preserved and material that has constitutional authority.

It prevents visual, procedural, or conversational approval from being mistaken for canon.

## Constitutional Role

Canon Boundary protects The Gradient from canon drift.

It preserves the authority of explicit constitutional ratification.

It allows material to be useful, beautiful, validated, promoted, or historically important without becoming constitutional by accident.

## Boundaries

Canon Boundary marks the edge around constitutional authority.

It is not Canon itself.

It is not Admission.

It is not Validation.

It is not Promotion.

It is not Archive.

It is not image approval.

It is not aesthetic success.

It is not popularity.

It does not move material across the boundary by itself.

Only explicit constitutional ratification can cross the canon boundary.

## Diagram Family

Boundary Plate.

Primary structure: one severe Canon Boundary dividing non-canonical institutional material from constitutional authority.

The boundary must show that useful, admitted, validated, promoted, and historical material remain outside canon unless routed to constitutional ratification.

Do not render Canon as already achieved.

Do not render a government border, religious relic, access-control screen, or mystical threshold.

## Visual Center

One severe central boundary labeled `CANON BOUNDARY`.

The visual center should make the boundary itself more important than the materials on either side.

Routes toward constitutional ratification may be visible, but crossing must not appear automatic.

The plate must communicate restraint, not elevation.

## Orientation

Landscape or near-square is acceptable.

Landscape may help show boundary separation.

The orientation reason is constitutional boundary clarity, not border politics, security control, or gate spectacle.
