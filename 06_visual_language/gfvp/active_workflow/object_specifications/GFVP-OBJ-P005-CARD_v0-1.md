## Purpose

The Card explains the human-facing knowledge object in The Gradient.

It is the form through which a steward can hold, revisit, annotate, and use metabolized material without directly manipulating internal Claims or Relationships.

## Constitutional Role

The Card protects human usability inside a complex knowledge ecology.

It gives experience, interpretation, practice, evidence, and source memory a workable institutional surface.

It preserves the distinction between human-facing use and internal reasoning structure.

## Boundaries

The Card follows metabolization.

It is a derived institutional object.

It is not raw source material.

It is not an admitted artifact.

It is not Source Review.

It is not a Claim.

It is not a Relationship.

It is not a folder.

It is not a database row.

It is not Canon.

It is not Validation.

It is not Promotion.

It does not make its contents true by existing.

It presents bounded, labeled material for human use and future institutional reasoning.

## Diagram Family

Object Anatomy Plate.

Primary structure: one central human-facing card object.

Secondary structure: small tether lines to source, claims, relationships, practice, and history.

Do not use a full knowledge graph.

Do not use a folder tree.

Do not use a database schema.

Do not use an app interface.

Do not use a dashboard.

Do not use a kanban board.

## Visual Center

One severe central rectangular institutional card labeled `CARD`.

The card contains the required card fields as internal rows or compartments.

Small external tethers connect the card to:

- Source Link
- Related Claims
- Related Relationships
- Practice Context
- History

The tethers must show relationship without making the card look like a network map.

The card must feel like a constitutional knowledge object, not a software UI card.

The visual hierarchy must make the Card more important than its tethers.

## Orientation

Portrait or near-square is preferred.

Landscape is not preferred unless the card anatomy becomes unreadable.

The orientation reason is object anatomy and human-facing readability, not threshold crossing, ledger readability, or conversion logic.
