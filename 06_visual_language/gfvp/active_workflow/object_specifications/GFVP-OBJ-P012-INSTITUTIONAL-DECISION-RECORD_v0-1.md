## Purpose

Institutional Decision Record explains the durable governance record that preserves consequential decisions.

It records the decision, rationale, alternatives, evidence, consequences, status, owner, and review trigger so institutional reasoning remains inspectable.

## Constitutional Role

Institutional Decision Records protect The Gradient from authority without memory.

They preserve why a consequential choice was made and when it should be reconsidered.

They make governance legible without turning every decision into canon.

## Boundaries

An Institutional Decision Record records a consequential decision and its reasoning.

It is not the Decision object itself.

It is not History, though it links to History.

It is not Canon.

It is not constitutional ratification.

It is not Validation.

It is not Promotion.

It is not a legal contract.

It is not a corporate policy memo.

It does not prove final truth.

It does not make a system work in practice.

It preserves governance reasoning for future review.

## Visual Center

One severe central object labeled `INSTITUTIONAL DECISION RECORD`.

The central ledger should contain the required governance fields in a stable, inspectable order.

The visual hierarchy must make the IDR ledger dominant and all tethers subordinate.

## Orientation

Portrait or near-square is preferred.

Landscape is acceptable only if the ledger fields become unreadable.

The orientation reason is governance ledger anatomy, not a software table or compliance dashboard.
