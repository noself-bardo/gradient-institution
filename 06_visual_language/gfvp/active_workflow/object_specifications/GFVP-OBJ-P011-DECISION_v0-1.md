## Purpose

Decision explains the smallest bounded governance object in The Gradient: a recorded choice.

It captures what was chosen, what was not chosen, why the choice was made, what evidence or constraints shaped it, and when it should be reviewed.

## Constitutional Role

Decision protects The Gradient from invisible authority.

It makes choice inspectable before the choice hardens into policy, doctrine, workflow, or institutional memory.

It preserves the difference between choosing a path and proving that the path is final, validated, promoted, or canonical.

## Boundaries

Decision records a choice.

It is not an Institutional Decision Record.

It is not History.

It is not Validation.

It is not Promotion.

It is not Canon.

It is not constitutional ratification.

It does not make an object true.

It does not prove an object works.

It does not automatically bind future stewards forever.

It may later be expanded into an Institutional Decision Record when the choice has durable governance consequence.

## Diagram Family

Governance Object Plate.

Primary structure: one central Decision object with a clear choice-point core.

Secondary structure: option inputs enter from one side, selected path exits from the other side, and review/history tethers remain subordinate.

Do not render a courtroom, ballot, corporate dashboard, kanban board, approval UI, or software workflow.

## Visual Center

One severe central object labeled `DECISION`.

The visual center should contain the core fields: Choice Point, Options Considered, Selected Path, Rationale, Consequence Notes, Review Trigger, and Status.

Rejected Alternatives should remain visible as subordinate side paths, not hidden or erased.

The plate must communicate bounded institutional choice, not authority, canon, validation, or promotion.

## Orientation

Portrait or near-square is preferred.

Landscape is acceptable only if option comparison becomes unreadable.

The orientation reason is governance-object anatomy, not a broad workflow.

## QC Requirements

Pass only if:

- the visual center is one Decision object
- selected path and rejected alternatives are both visible
- rationale and review trigger are present
- Decision does not appear as canon, validation, promotion, or constitutional ratification
- the diagram does not become a corporate decision tree, software approval UI, legal verdict, or project-management board
