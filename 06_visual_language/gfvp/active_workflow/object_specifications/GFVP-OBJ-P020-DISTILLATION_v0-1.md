## Purpose

Distillation extracts a durable reduced form from larger living material while keeping source shadow, omission, and use limits visible.

## Constitutional Role

It matters because The Gradient needs durable forms that carry essential structure without pretending to metabolize raw sources, summarize everything, canonize, publish, or simplify away complexity.

## Boundaries

Distillation is not Metabolization: metabolization converts reviewed source material into institutional knowledge objects, while distillation reduces existing living material into a durable form. It is not summarization: it preserves source shadow and omission limits rather than merely shortening text. It is not canonization: distilled material has no constitutional authority by being compressed. It is not publication: durable form is not release. It is not simplification: complexity may remain marked rather than erased.
