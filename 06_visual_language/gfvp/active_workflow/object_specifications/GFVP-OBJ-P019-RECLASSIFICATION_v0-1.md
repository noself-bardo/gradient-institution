## Purpose

Reclassification changes the category or status assigned to material while retaining the prior meaning, trace, and reason for change.

## Constitutional Role

It matters because The Gradient must be able to move material between categories without deleting it, falsely rejecting it, promoting it, validating it, canonizing it, or rewriting what it meant.

## Boundaries

Reclassification is not rejection: it changes category without necessarily discarding material. It is not promotion: movement may be lateral, downward, or clarifying. It is not validation: a changed category does not prove usefulness. It is not canonization: no constitutional authority is granted. It is not revision: the material itself is not rewritten; its classification, status, or category relation changes while prior meaning remains traceable.
