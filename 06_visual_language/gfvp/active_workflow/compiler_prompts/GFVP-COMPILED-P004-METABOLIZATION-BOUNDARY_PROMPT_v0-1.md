Object Name: Metabolization Boundary.

Purpose: The Metabolization Boundary explains the threshold between reviewed source material and derived institutional knowledge objects. It shows that an admitted and reviewed source may be transformed into Cards, Claims, Frames, Practices, or Relationships without becoming validated, promoted, or canonical by default.

Constitutional Role: The Metabolization Boundary protects The Gradient from confusing interpretation with authority. It allows useful institutional knowledge work to begin while preserving the distinction between source, interpretation, validation, promotion, and canon. It prevents raw material, admitted material, or source-reviewed material from being treated as durable knowledge before it has been structurally transformed and clearly labeled.

Boundaries: Metabolization follows Admission Gate and Source Review. It is not Admission Gate. It is not Source Review. It is not Validation. It is not Promotion. It is not Canonization. It does not decide final truth. It does not approve an engine. It does not make a claim canonical. It converts reviewed material into bounded institutional knowledge objects for later review, use, validation, or rejection.
