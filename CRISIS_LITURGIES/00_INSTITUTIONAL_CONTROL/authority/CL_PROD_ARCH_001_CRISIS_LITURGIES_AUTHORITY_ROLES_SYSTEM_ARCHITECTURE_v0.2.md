# 1. Purpose

This document defines the institutional and technical architecture required to produce, repair, validate, assemble, freeze, publish, and archive Crisis Liturgies without relying on accumulated chat history, overlapping agent personas, undocumented folder state, or repeated manual intervention.

Its purpose is to establish:

1. a stable authority hierarchy;
2. clear role boundaries;
3. a canonical source-of-truth model;
4. a deterministic production state machine;
5. a bounded role for Chachipti;
6. distinct responsibilities for ChatGPT, Codex, Cursor, Notion, Google Drive, Git, and the local repository;
7. a non-destructive corrective lane;
8. machine-readable production contracts;
9. a tested path from approved concept to frozen release;
10. a ratification sequence that prevents premature locking.

This document does not declare any disputed volume complete, released, publicly verified, canonically admitted, or superseded.

# 2. Governing conclusion

The Chachipti packet is not adopted as an independent constitution.

Chachipti is provisionally designated as a **specification compiler and corrective packet engine** operating beneath Crisis Liturgies Canon, accepted Canon Change Reports, the Crisis Liturgies Generation Standard, approved release profiles, and frozen volume specifications.

The production system will not place constitutional authority, archive authority, prompt compilation, execution management, release-state declaration, and public publication control in one agent.

The system will instead use a layered separation of authority:

1. **Canon establishes meaning and invariant law.**
2. **Production standards establish compliant methodology.**
3. **Release profiles establish permitted volume forms.**
4. **Frozen volume specifications establish the complete approved definition of one volume.**
5. **Chachipti compiles approved specifications into execution packets.**
6. **The deterministic builder validates, renders, repairs, assembles, and freezes.**
7. **Cursor and Codex execute bounded repository work.**
8. **Quality-control systems determine conformity.**
9. **The Archivist records provenance, admission, and publication state.**
10. **The human founder ratifies genuine institutional decisions and final admissions.**

No lower layer may revise a higher layer.

# 3. Architectural principles

## 3.1 One institution, one operational root

All active Crisis Liturgies production must occur within one canonical project root.

No approved production process may depend on:

- Desktop folders;
- Downloads folders;
- undocumented local folders;
- arbitrary Google Drive locations;
- loose chat attachments;
- temporary agent workspaces;
- duplicated active release trees;
- linked assets outside the canonical project.

Historical artifacts may remain in legacy locations temporarily during migration, but they must be registered, checksummed, classified, and made read-only before the legacy root is retired.

## 3.2 Chat is not production state

Chat may develop concepts, surface contradictions, review work, and record human decisions.

Chat history may not serve as:

- a builder input;
- a render packet;
- a release manifest;
- a volume specification;
- a current-state ledger;
- a substitute for ratified canon;
- a substitute for a repository artifact.

A human decision made in chat becomes operational only after it is captured in the appropriate institutional or machine-readable record.

## 3.3 Frozen specifications govern builds

Every build must originate from a complete, versioned, validated, and checksummed volume specification.

The builder may not infer missing decisions.

When a required field is missing, the correct result is a validation failure with an exact remediation request.

## 3.4 Frozen releases are immutable

A frozen release may not be modified in place.

Corrections, repairs, and aesthetic reconstruction must create:

- a corrective intake;
- a new execution namespace;
- preserved prior evidence;
- a candidate release;
- a new version or successor record;
- explicit human disposition.

## 3.5 Production authority is narrow and traceable

Every agent, tool, and system receives only the authority necessary for its function.

No agent may infer its own authority from access to files.

## 3.6 Operational state must be machine-verifiable

Folder names and prose claims do not establish production state.

A file located in a folder named `FINAL` is not automatically a frozen release.

A public URL is not automatically an authorized public release.

Every state transition must be represented in an approved state record and supported by required manifests.

## 3.7 Implementations remain replaceable

Canon and production contracts should survive replacement of:

- Cursor;
- Codex;
- a specific model;
- an image provider;
- a layout engine;
- a programming language;
- a hosting provider;
- an interface.

The institution must not be structurally dependent on one vendor or application.

# 4. Authority hierarchy

The following hierarchy governs all Crisis Liturgies production:

1. **Crisis Liturgies Canon**
2. **Accepted Canon Change Reports**
3. **Locked Crisis Liturgies Generation Standard**
4. **Approved release profile**
5. **Frozen volume specification**
6. **Current state record and build lock**
7. **Approved issue, page, and render specifications**
8. **Locked editorial, source, prompt, symbol, and typography registers**
9. **Quality-control and repair records**
10. **Job-specific handoffs and runbooks**
11. **Derived assets and generated output**
12. **Chat history and historical provenance**

When two records conflict, the higher authority governs.

When two records at the same authority level conflict:

1. the newest human-ratified record governs if it explicitly supersedes the earlier record;
2. otherwise production stops;
3. a conflict record is opened;
4. no downstream agent may choose a preferred interpretation.

Model output has no authority to revise upstream specifications.

# 5. Institutional roles

The human founder retains authority over:

- canon changes;
- concept approval;
- render approval;
- disputed interpretation;
- final visual judgment;
- archive admission;
- public release authorization;
- corrective relock;

The Constitutional Steward determines whether a proposed change belongs in:

- canon;
- governance;
- production standards;
- a release profile;
- a volume specification;
- the archive.

The Constitutional Steward may propose changes but may not ratify them without explicit human authorization.

## 5.3 Crisis Liturgies Archivist

The Archivist owns:

- canonical collection identity;
- issue and volume registries;
- provenance;
- archive classifications;
- release-state evidence;
- frozen-release admission;
- supersession records;
- public-release records;
- historical exceptions;
- storage-mirror verification.

The Archivist does not generate images, rewrite volume content, or declare conformity without QC evidence.

## 5.4 Crisis Liturgies Conversion Architect

The Conversion Architect owns failed or disputed production intake.

Responsibilities include:

- chain of custody;
- source arbitration;
- failure autopsy;
- canon recovery;
- reconstruction architecture;
- page rebuild mapping;
- guardrail specification;
- render-contract preparation;
- production-handoff preparation;
- post-render validation.

The Conversion Architect does not grant archive admission or public release.

## 5.5 Chachipti Specification Compiler

Provisional system designation:

**CL-COMPILER-001 — Chachipti Specification Compiler and Corrective Packet Engine**

Chachipti may:

- inspect a frozen volume specification;
- validate specification completeness;

- map a release profile to invariant page functions;
- compile issue and page specifications;
- compile render packets;
- compile layout instructions;
- emit exact Cursor work orders;
- emit corrective intake and repair packets;

Chachipti may not:

- rewrite canon;
- create a new release profile without authorization;
- invent unresolved volume content;
- change approved copy;
- reorder issues;
- change designations;
- authorize image generation;
- declare a release frozen;
- declare a release public;
- overwrite a frozen release;
- resolve conflicting human decisions silently.

## 5.6 Deterministic Builder

The builder is software, not a persona.

It owns:

- schema validation;
- path validation;
- manifest generation;
- build locking;
- render-adapter invocation;
- automated QC;
- retry and repair orchestration;
- deterministic layout;
- PDF assembly;
- checksum generation;
- candidate-release creation;
- freeze preparation;
- reproducibility reporting.

The builder must be idempotent where technically possible and must expose exact failure reasons.

Codex is the preferred engineering agent for:

- builder implementation;
- validators;
- schemas;
- automated tests;
- repository scripts;
- migration utilities;
- repair automation;
- continuous integration;
- reproducible build tooling.

Codex may modify code and infrastructure within an approved task scope.

Codex does not determine canon, volume state, or archive admission.

Cursor is the preferred repository execution environment for:

- controlled local execution;
- file inspection;
- script execution;
- layout repair;
- linked-asset repair;
- repository diffs;
- candidate assembly;
- exact job packets produced by Chachipti or the Conversion Architect.

ChatGPT is the human-facing interpretive and development environment.

It may support:

- concept development;
- editorial review;
- conflict analysis;
- QC reasoning;
- production planning;
- ratification drafting;

Notion is the human-readable institutional index.

It should contain:

- current institutional status;
- ratified decisions;
- readable canon;
- registries;
- links to repository artifacts;

Notion is not the sole runtime source for the builder.

Google Drive is the institutional storage and distribution mirror for:

- frozen print masters;
- frozen screen derivatives;
- release snapshots;
- storage mirrors;
- archive packages;
- public-release assets;
- checksum manifests.

Drive should not contain unmanaged active working copies across unrelated roots.

## 5.12 Git repository

Git is the version-control and change-audit layer for:

- schemas;
- builder code;
- specifications;
- manifests;
- textual source files;
- configuration;
- status records;
- migration records;

Large binary assets may be mirrored outside Git when required, but their checksums and authoritative locations must remain registered.

# 10. Production state machine

The proposed state machine is:

```text
CONCEPT_DRAFT
→ CONCEPT_APPROVED
→ SPECIFICATION_IN_PROGRESS
→ SPECIFICATION_COMPLETE
→ BUILD_VALIDATED
→ APPROVED_FOR_RENDER
→ RENDERING
→ AUTOMATED_QC
→ REPAIR_QUEUE
→ ASSEMBLY
→ FINAL_QC
→ RELEASE_CANDIDATE
→ FROZEN_RELEASE
→ PUBLICATION_AUTHORIZED
→ PUBLICLY_RELEASED
```

Additional classifications:

```text
QUARANTINED
SUPERSEDED
WITHDRAWN
HISTORICAL_REFERENCE
CORRECTIVE_REOPEN
CORRECTIVE_CANDIDATE
```

## 10.1 State distinctions

- `SPECIFICATION_COMPLETE` means content is complete, not render-authorized.
- `BUILD_VALIDATED` means schemas and dependencies pass, not that visual output is approved.
- `RELEASE_CANDIDATE` is not a frozen release.
- `FROZEN_RELEASE` is not automatically public.
- `PUBLICATION_AUTHORIZED` precedes deployment.
- `PUBLICLY_RELEASED` requires verification of the actual published asset.
- `CORRECTIVE_CANDIDATE` never silently replaces a prior frozen or public release.

## 10.2 Invalid transitions

The builder must reject:

- `CONCEPT_DRAFT → RENDERING`;
- `SPECIFICATION_COMPLETE → PUBLICLY_RELEASED`;
- `RELEASE_CANDIDATE → PUBLICLY_RELEASED` without freeze and publication authorization;
- any modification of `FROZEN_RELEASE` in place;
- any state claim lacking required manifests;
- any release using unresolved external assets.

The builder must reject:

- absolute paths outside the canonical root;

- undocumented Drive shortcuts;
- external font dependencies without an inventory and license record;
- outputs written directly into a frozen-release directory;

# 14. Production lanes

## 14.1 Lane A: Standard production

Use for new or active volumes.

Sequence:

```text
concept
→ approved release profile
→ volume specification
→ specification QC
→ frozen specification
→ build validation
→ render approval
→ packet compilation
→ rendering
→ automated QC
→ bounded repair
→ assembly
→ final QC
→ release candidate
→ archive admission
→ publication authorization
```

## 14.2 Lane B: Corrective production

Use for a locked, closed, released, or human-declared nonconforming artifact.

Mandatory properties:

- bounded scope;
- named failure classes;
- preserved prior evidence;
- prior-asset checksums;
- namespaced corrective outputs;
- no overwrite of frozen releases;
- candidate-only status until human closure.

Approved closure decisions:

- `CORRECTIVE_RELOCK_AUTHORIZED`
- `CORRECTIVE_PUBLIC_REPLACE_AUTHORIZED`
- `CORRECTIVE_ABORT_RESTORE_PRIOR`

The existing Chachipti Corrective Lane Protocol is admitted as the basis for this lane, subject to removal of the conflicting builder trigger-word gate and alignment with the authority hierarchy in this document.

## 14.3 Lane C: Conversion and migration

Use for:

- disputed state;
- broken lineage;
- incomplete source packages;
- generic editorial drift;
- missing editable sources;
- source-authority collisions;
- historical material entering the new architecture.

Owned by the Conversion Architect.

Lane C ends by producing one of:

- an admissible frozen specification for Lane A;
- a bounded Corrective Intake for Lane B;
- a quarantine decision;
- a historical-reference classification;
- a request for human canon resolution.

# 16. Stop conditions

All production agents must stop when:

- canon sources conflict;
- a required approval is absent;
- an unresolved field would require invention;
- a path escapes the project root;
- a frozen release would be overwritten;
- a source-sensitive claim lacks evidence;
- linked assets are missing;
- font inventory is incomplete;
- release status is disputed;
- a new volume number is being inferred rather than assigned;
- a page profile is undeclared;
- retry ceilings are exceeded;
- automated QC cannot produce a clear result;
- a requested correction requires a canon change.

Every stop must produce:

1. the exact blocking condition;
2. the affected artifact;
3. the governing rule;
4. the smallest required human decision or source repair;
5. the state in which work was safely halted.

# Appendix A — Ratified named patches

## NP-001 — Canonical Root and Controlled Mirrors

**Affects:** L-001, L-006, L-010, L-011

One logical canonical operational root governs active Crisis Liturgies production. Read-only release mirrors, storage mirrors, backups, temporary registered run workspaces, and publication derivatives are permitted only when their relationship to the canonical root is explicit, manifest-backed, and incapable of becoming a competing active production root.

## NP-002 — Institutional Authority Preservation

**Affects:** L-003, L-007, L-011

The repository is the machine-readable operational source, but it does not supersede Crisis Liturgies Canon, accepted Canon Change Reports, or locked production standards. Chachipti and the builder must compile and execute higher authority rather than reinterpret it.

## NP-003 — Bounded Chachipti Authority

**Affects:** L-007, L-016

Chachipti is ratified only as a specification compiler and corrective packet engine. It may validate, normalize, compile, and stop. It may not invent unresolved content, change approved copy, grant render authorization, admit an artifact to the archive, declare publication, or silently resolve authority conflicts.

## NP-004 — Tool-Agnostic Execution

**Affects:** L-008

Cursor and Codex are the current preferred execution environments. Their names and present responsibilities are operational choices, not permanent constitutional dependencies. A future executor may replace either tool if it conforms to the same bounded contracts, path rules, validation requirements, and authority limits.

## NP-005 — Registry and Mirror Boundaries

**Affects:** L-009, L-010, L-011

Notion communicates ratified status and institutional context. Drive preserves and distributes frozen artifacts and derivatives. The repository carries machine-readable active production state. None of these systems may silently create a new state merely because a file, page, or folder appears there.

## NP-006 — Dual Image-Authorization Domains

**Affects:** L-012, L-013

The durable builder uses CONCEPT_APPROVED and APPROVED_FOR_RENDER as its production gates. The founder's exact standalone INTIFADA convention remains in force for interactive native ChatGPT image generation. The interactive convention does not interrupt or replace an already authorized deterministic builder run. Archive admission, final visual judgment, and publication authorization remain separate human decisions, not repeated per-asset render gates.

## NP-007 — Legacy Release Protection

**Affects:** L-004, L-005, L-014

Existing frozen releases remain immutable even when their historical documentation predates the new manifest standard. Retrospective manifests, checksums, QC summaries, and provenance records may be attached without altering the frozen binary. A correction must create a namespaced candidate and, if admitted, a new version or successor record.

## NP-008 — Disputed-State Evidence Threshold

**Affects:** L-015

Conflicting or incomplete state claims must be classified as DISPUTED, UNVERIFIED, or UNKNOWN with evidence and confidence recorded. Absence from one repository is not proof of nonexistence. No agent may promote uncertainty into an affirmative release, archive, or publication state.

## NP-009 — Recommendation Is Not Ratification

**Affects:** L-016

Agents and automated systems may recommend a state transition and produce supporting evidence. They may not self-ratify. Archive admission and public-release authorization require the recorded human decision and the Archivist's corresponding institutional record.

## NP-010 — Non-Destructive Migration

**Affects:** L-001, L-004, L-006, L-010, L-014

No legacy source, release, or mirror may be moved, deleted, renamed, or retired until its checksum, provenance, replacement path, and recovery route have been verified. Migration proceeds by copy, validation, registration, and only then controlled retirement.
